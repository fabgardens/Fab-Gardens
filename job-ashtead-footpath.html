🌻 Ashtead Village Public Footpath

Complete restoration of a neglected public footpath

Ashtead, Surrey
January 2025
Completed

Transformation Video

Project Overview

This public footpath in Ashtead Village had become dangerously overgrown, forcing pedestrians to either walk in the road or avoid the route entirely. The vegetation had encroached significantly onto the pathway, creating hazards for walkers, families, and especially those with mobility challenges.

Fab Gardens was nominated to restore this vital community walkway, documenting the entire process to raise awareness about the importance of maintaining our public rights of way.

Risk Assessment

Before undertaking any work, a thorough risk assessment was conducted to ensure safety throughout the project.

Identified Hazards

Hazard Risk Level Control Measures Residual Risk
Sharp tools (secateurs, loppers) Medium PPE gloves, proper handling, secure storage Low
Hidden debris/sharp objects High Protective footwear, careful clearance, visual inspection Controlled
Thorny vegetation (brambles) Medium Long sleeves, thick gloves, eye protection Low
Uneven ground/trip hazards Medium Sturdy boots, careful movement, clear as you go Low
Passing traffic (adjacent road) Medium High-vis clothing, awareness, work away from road edge Controlled
Manual handling strain Low Regular breaks, proper lifting technique Low

PPE Used

  • Safety gloves (cut-resistant)
  • Safety boots (steel toe cap)
  • High-visibility vest
  • Safety glasses
  • Long-sleeved clothing

Emergency Procedures

First aid kit on site. Emergency contact numbers recorded. Nearest A&E: Epsom Hospital (15 minutes). Mobile phone fully charged for emergencies.

Safety Outcome

This footpath is now fully accessible and safe for all pedestrians. Families with pushchairs, elderly residents, and dog walkers can now use this route without being forced into the road. The community has regained a vital walking connection.

Community Impact

This restoration directly benefits:

  • Local residents using the footpath daily
  • Families with young children and pushchairs
  • Elderly residents who rely on safe walking routes
  • Dog walkers in the area
  • Anyone with mobility challenges who needs clear pathways
